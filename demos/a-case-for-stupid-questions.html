A Case for Stupid Questions

Everyone has heard it said that "there is no such thing as a stupid question".

I can think of a class of question that defies this notion.

It's not the kind of question that is not worth asking because the answer is obvious, or the asker is uninformed.

It's the type of question where the answer does more harm than good.

Maybe it's me, or maybe it's human nature, but of course I need to ask these stupid questions anyway.

In my core I value truth over pragmatism or utility, and so I ask these questions often.

I don't think many of you are different, and I doubt you would avoid asking a question based on the pretence that it is destructive.

To walk away from a question like this, you would need to trust my assessment that it is not good for people to ask it, and you would need to leave this webpage now.

These questions don't raise inconvenient truths we would be better off acknowledging.

These are truths we would be better off not contemplating at all.

So here's my stupid question:

To be the among the best in the world at something, do you need to work yourself sick?

The reason this is stupid is precisely because the question is valid, and one of the possible answers is harmful and problematic.

Some significant part of me believes I would never have made it as far as I have as a developer and a founder if I hadn't sacrificed some mental health.

Many of time's greatest minds would be deemed mentally ill in present-day, would we prefer if Leonardo Davinci was normal, or "healthy"?

I wouldn't.

Yet I wouldn't wish the mental health problems I imposed on myself on anyone else.

There are tons of people who have "made it" who preach that you should just relax, have a balanced lifestyle and take care of yourself.

Did they relax when they were coming up?

Do they even really relax now? If they do, is it because they only now have that luxury?

Can I in good conscience recommend someone live a balanced lifestyle if I didn't? If I don't?

When someone asks how I got where I am, should I admit that my path involved all-nighters, isolation, hospitalization and chemicals?

Do you need to expose yourself to harm to excel? I don't know, it's a stupid question.
